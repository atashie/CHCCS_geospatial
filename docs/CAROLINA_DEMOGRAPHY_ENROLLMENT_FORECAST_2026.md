## Three Iterative Projection Layers

The report builds forecasts incrementally, each layer adding more data:

1. **Baseline (GPR only)** — Grade Progression Ratio model using 10 years of historical ADM by grade by school. Kindergarten forecasted via birth-to-kindergarten ratios using births 5 years prior. Time-series methods used where GPRs show trends (not just averages).

2. **Baseline + Development Yield** — Adds net new students from 42 residential/mixed-use developments (6,825 total units, 6,653 non-age-restricted). This is the **primary forecast**. Key: they estimate *net new* students (not total from developments) to avoid double-counting students already captured in GPR trends.

3. **Facilities Utilization Overlay** — Maps the development-adjusted forecast against each school's reported capacity to show which schools are over/under-utilized. Not a separate ADM projection — same numbers viewed through a capacity lens.

Development yield recovers 566 students vs. baseline, reducing the 10-year decline from -1,637 to -1,070.

### Key Drivers of Decline

- **Declining births:** COVID-era birth dip (2020-21) now hitting kindergarten cohorts; cascading through grades
- **Falling birth-to-K ratio:** Was 1.18 in 2015-16 (net in-migration of families), now declining — fewer families moving in with young children
- **Reduced international migration:** ELL headcount dropped from 1,621 to 1,413 this year (first post-pandemic decrease), attributed to federal immigration policy changes
- **School choice expansion:** Opportunity Scholarship (voucher) program tripling in funding 2026-27; charter growth. CHCCS market share increased 7pp post-COVID rebound but faces downward pressure
- **Enrollment capture rate declining:** 94% to 91% of resident 5-17 year olds attending CHCCS (2020 to 2024)

### Historical Elementary Context

- Elementary schools absorbed the largest absolute decline over the past decade: -1,207 students (5,501 to 4,294) since 2015-16
- Morris Grove saw the largest 10-year loss (-186 students)
- Northside and Rashkis both saw ~15% year-over-year declines in the current year

## Development Probability & Occupancy Methodology

### Status-Based Probability of Completion

| Status | Probability |
|---|---|
| Under construction (field-verified near complete) | 95-100% |
| Final Plans Review | 85% |
| Entitled | 75% |
| Formal Application Review | 60% |
| Concept Plan Complete | 40% |
| Waiting for Formal Dev Application Submittal | 30% |

Where site visits indicate units are already occupied, those units are removed from the forecast to avoid double-counting with baseline ADM.

### Occupancy Ramp Schedule (% of units occupied by forecast year)

| Year | Complete/Near | Under Construction | Final Plans | Entitled | Formal App Review | Concept Plan | Waiting for App |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2026 | 80% | 0% | 0% | 0% | 0% | 0% | 0% |
| 2027 | 100% | 30% | 0% | 0% | 0% | 0% | 0% |
| 2028 | 100% | 50% | 30% | 20% | 0% | 0% | 0% |
| 2029 | 100% | 80% | 50% | 50% | 30% | 0% | 0% |
| 2030 | 100% | 100% | 80% | 80% | 50% | 20% | 10% |
| 2031 | 100% | 100% | 100% | 100% | 80% | 50% | 30% |
| 2032+ | 100% | 100% | 100% | 100% | 100% | 80%+ | 60%+ |

### Student Yield Calculation Pipeline

1. Map developments to Orange County parcels (area-weighted allocation for multi-parcel projects)
2. Join parcels to school attendance zones
3. Apply completion probability by project status
4. Apply project-specific occupancy ramp schedule
5. Apply tract-level occupancy rates from 2020 Census (share of housing typically occupied vs. vacant/short-term rental)
6. Apply tract-level age-group household rates (% of households with children ages 5-10 for elementary, 11-13 for middle, 14-17 for high)
7. Apply market share adjustments (not all public-school eligible children will enroll in CHCCS)
8. Subtract students already captured in GPR baseline trends to produce net new yield

## Methodological Notes

### Difference from SAPFOTAC

The Carolina Demography approach differs fundamentally from the previous SAPFOTAC methodology:

- **SAPFOTAC** estimated *total potential students* per development
- **Carolina Demography** estimates *net new students* above what the GPR baseline already captures
- Per-development yields are therefore **systematically lower** than SAPFOTAC's — by design, not error
- Report footnote (doc page 19): "This is different from the goals of reports such as those from SAPFOTAC where we are looking at the potential total impact of each development instead of the net impact as we are."

### Data Sources (CHCCS)

| Dataset | Source | Years Covered |
|---|---|---|
| Average Daily Membership Month 2 (by grade by school) | CHCCS, NCDPI | 2015-16 through 2025-26 |
| Attendance Zone Boundaries | CHCCS | 2025-26 |
| Decennial Census (population by age, housing occupancy) | US Census Bureau | 2000, 2010, 2020 |
| Population Estimates and Forecasts (by age) | NC OSBM | 2010 through 2060 |
| Births to resident mothers | NC DPH | 2011 through 2022 |
| Approved/Proposed Developments | Chapel Hill & Carrboro municipalities | Current (March 2026) |

### Key Demographic Inputs

- **School-age population (SAIPE):** 12,678 (2021) declining to 12,309 (2024)
- **Enrollment capture rate:** 94% (2020) declining to 91% (2024)
- **Birth-to-kindergarten ratio:** 1.18 in 2015-16, now declining toward/below 1.0 — structural shift from net importer to potential net exporter of school-age families
- **ELL headcount:** 1,621 (2024-25) to 1,413 (2025-26) — first post-pandemic decrease, attributed to federal immigration policy
- **OSBM school-age projection:** 18,369 ages 5-17 in Orange County in 2025; declining to ~17,500 by 2036

## Observations Relevant to CHCCS Geospatial Project

### Closure Analysis Context
- Northside (51% capacity), Rashkis (45%), and Scroggs (56%) are the most underutilized by 2035-36 — even after accounting for all known development
- These are the schools most frequently discussed in closure scenarios

### Receiving School Capacity Constraints
- Glenwood exceeds 100% capacity by 2030-31 even without absorbing closure students
- FPG stays near 95% throughout — very limited absorption capacity
- Ephesus reaches 97% by 2033-34 — similarly constrained
- McDougle (90%), Estes Hills (78%), Carrboro (75%) have some headroom

### Development Data
- The 42-development list (March 2026) is more current than the SAPFOTAC 2025 Annual Report data
- Includes field-verified status adjustments and probability weighting by status
- Could be cross-referenced against project data in `data/raw/properties/planned/`
